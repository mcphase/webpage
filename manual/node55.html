Going beyond the Dipole Approximation for the Neutron Form factor

Usually the magnetic form factor is calculated by the dipole approximation (see appendix J). In order to go beyond the dipole approximation (see [32] chapter 11.6.2,equations 11.141-11.143, mind: there is a small error in equ 11.141 and 11.142 in this book, see below) for the magnetic form factor it is necessary to take into account the expectation values and (in addition to and ). Moreover the ground state wave functions, the -coefficients (see appendix K or [32],table 11.1) and the momentum quantum number for each magnetic ion is required. This information has to be given in the input file in the following format:
